📋 Common Health Risks
🦟 Dengue Fever
High risk during monsoon (June–October). Symptoms: high fever, severe headache, joint pain, rash. Seek care immediately.
💧 Cholera / Water-Borne Illness
Common after flooding. Drink only filtered/boiled water. Symptoms: watery diarrhoea, dehydration.
☀️ Heat Stroke
Peak summer (April–June). Stay hydrated, avoid direct sun 11 AM–4 PM. If confused or unconscious, call 112 immediately.
🌫️ Air Pollution / Respiratory Issues
AQI often high in urban areas. Asthma patients should carry inhalers. Excellent pulmonology units available.

📋 Emergency First Steps
1. Stay calm and assess the situation safely.
2. Call 112 (all emergencies) or 102 (ambulance).
3. Give your exact location and describe the emergency clearly.
4. Don't move an injured person unless in immediate danger.
5. If breathing has stopped and you're trained, start CPR.
6. Keep the person warm, conscious and reassured.
7. Clear the area and send someone to guide emergency services.
